### Post 8
---
Hot take: I think everyone who applies for a job should pay a $25 application fee.
Before you get too mad, know that it gets worse.

If you get referred for a job, both the applicant and the person making the referral should pay $100. If the referral comes from a recruiter, both the candidate and recruiter should pay $250.

Now here’s the part that matters: that money should not just disappear.
If you pass the phone screen, you get all your money back.
If the role is filled before you get to that point, you get all your money back.
If you make it past the first interview after the phone screen, you should get twice your money back. Every round after that should double again.

Why?

Because the job market is broken on both sides.
Candidates are applying to 70, 80, 90+ jobs because the process feels like a lottery. AI auto-applying tools are only going to make that worse. Real stakes would force applicants to be more intentional and make sure they meet at least the basic requirements before applying.
But companies should also have real stakes.

Interviewing takes time. If a company asks someone to go through multiple rounds, assignments, panels, and conversations, that person should be compensated for their time.
Referrals should also carry more responsibility. Employees and recruiters making referrals should be held to a higher standard and have more skin in the game, but they should also share in the reward when the candidate advances.

The company should be held accountable, too.
If they do not respond within a week of the application closing, they should be penalized. Maybe $10 per candidate.
If they reject candidates because of bias, discrimination, or another invalid reason, the penalty should be much higher. I’d add a zero to the application fee.

To keep everyone honest, the money should be held in escrow, and any claims should be adjudicated by a neutral third party. I know this is controversial, but right now, the hiring process has almost no accountability for the people and companies with the most power.

Candidates are expected to absorb the time, cost, silence, and rejection with no compensation and no transparency.
Maybe money is not the perfect answer, but the current system clearly does not value anyone’s time equally.

What do you think?
