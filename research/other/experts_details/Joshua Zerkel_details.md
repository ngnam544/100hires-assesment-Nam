About

I work at the intersection of community and go-to-market strategy.

My focus is designing how community integrates into product, marketing, sales, and customer success in ways that drive measurable business outcomes.

I call this Community-Integrated GTM.

Community isn’t a channel; it’s infrastructure. When embedded intentionally, it influences adoption, retention, expansion, product insights, and durable competitive advantage.

For more than 20 years, I’ve built and scaled global community ecosystems across B2B and B2C SaaS. The throughline is integration: connecting customers to each other, translating signal back into the business, and aligning community efforts with revenue and product priorities.

I currently serve as Head of Marketing & Community at Gradual, where I lead marketing execution and ensure community and education are structurally aligned with our broader GTM strategy.

I’m the author of The Community Code, where I explore what it looks like to embed community into operating models rather than treat it as a standalone initiative. Through writing and advisory work, I partner with founders and GTM leaders who want community to function as a strategic lever within their broader system.

Previously:

At Asana, I scaled the global community ecosystem to nearly 700,000 members and built the Ambassador program from 0 to 25,000 leaders across 190+ countries. Community members were 53% more likely to be on a paid plan, with a 40% lift in team collaboration. Programs influenced $94M+ in pipeline.

At Evernote, I led customer education, events, and engagement strategy for a global user base of 200M+, translating community insight into product feedback, advocacy, and revenue impact.
At HeyGen, I built community and customer education infrastructure from the ground up, integrating adoption and retention into GTM from day one.

If you’re thinking about how community intersects with product strategy, pipeline, expansion, or differentiation, that’s the work I focus on.
