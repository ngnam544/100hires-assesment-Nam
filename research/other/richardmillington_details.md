In early February I traveled to Germany for three radioligand imaging scans. This post explains what I did and why.

Background
In 2024, my cancer recurred and my doctors told me I had exhausted standard treatment options. This was when I took agency over my care. My team and I conducted a set of research diagnostic protocols that went far beyond what is typically done. From these analyses, we discovered my cancer overexpressed FAP, a fibroblast marker. My team made the connection to Richard Baum, a pioneer in nuclear medicine who has successfully treated patients with a radioligand therapy targeting FAP. In late 2024, I traveled to his lab at CURANOSTICUM Wiesbaden-Frankfurt for imaging, and then again in early 2025 for treatment. The treatment was so successful the tumor shrank enough to become surgically operable again, and I currently have no evidence of disease.

This experience revealed to me the power and importance of radioligand diagnostics. Standard PET imaging works by measuring metabolic activity (cancers require high metabolic activity to fuel unchecked growth), but radioligand imaging is tailored to a specific biomarker. It can show you not just if the biomarker is present, but also where it is expressed throughout your body. That matters for treatment planning: if we need to use a therapy targeting a particular biomarker, where else will it be taken up? What are the potential toxicities?
In many cases, you can swap the tracer element used for diagnostics out for a therapeutic payload. With my FAP-targeting treatment, doctors used the harmless Gallium-68 isotope for imaging and the cancer-destroying Lutetium-177 and Actinium-225 isotopes for treatment.
Now I'm systematically imaging other biomarkers we've identified through sequencing and tissue staining, building what I think of as a personal biodistribution map.

What I Did
I went to Germany for scans, including going to Essen for scans against multiple relevant biomarkers for which a specific imaging agent is available and going back to CURANOSTICUM for longitudinal scanning of FAP. These scans can reveal both target insights (such as where the biomarker is distributed in the body) and drug-specific insights (such as whether the molecule bioaccumulates in a tissue).
FAP imaging: FAP is expressed in inflammation and fibrosis, not just cancer, so continued tracking is important for recurrence monitoring. The image below is read from right to left, with the right-most image being from my first trip to CURANOSTICUM in October 2024 and the left-most image being from my most recent trip. The kidney bean-shaped yellow lesion in my spine was the cancer. Now it’s gone!

Results
No cancer detected on any scan.
I now have data on where agents targeting these biomarkers are taken up in my body. If my cancer returns expressing these markers, it helps me decide which therapies to consider and think through what the off-target effects might be.

A small hotel struggled to compete with the big chains...but a $20 phone that delivered popsicles to where you were sat TOTALLY changed the game...

It sounds ridiculous. But here's how it worked.

Ordinarily when people compete, they go head-to-head on features. The hotel approach to this is an expensive remodel of the rooms, but the problem wasn't the rooms, it was being memorable.

They decided to install a bright red landline phone by their pool. They called it "The Popsicle Hotline."

You didn’t use it to call the front desk. You picked it up, waited a moment, and a few minutes later, a white-gloved butler in a crisp suit would walk out carrying fresh popsicles on a silver platter.

That was it.

And yet, guests couldn’t stop talking about it.

They told friends. Family. Coworkers. It became the story people shared when they talked about their trip, and slowly...that little hotel became known for something far bigger than its size.

It ended up being a business phenomenon, proving that even the simplest, most inexpensive gestures can leave the biggest impressions on customers.

And the same goes for your product.

You don’t win people over by shouting about features. You win by making them feel seen.

What truly makes people advocate for you is a genuine, enjoyable experience that can come rather unexpectedly.

When a developer hits a problem, make a personal video guiding them through the solution. Notice someone in your community helping others? Send them a heartfelt thank-you message along with swag that fits their personality.

The best developer experiences aren’t built by doing more…they come from creating meaningful moments in the small, ordinary interactions.
