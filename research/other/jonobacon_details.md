If you build a COMMUNITY, you won’t become a COMMODITY

Systems eat motivation for breakfast…

Motivation is fleeting, like a spark that ignites a fire.

Without the right systems in place, that fire dies out pretty quickly.

In the past whenever I wanted to get into shape, I’d rely solely on motivation to pump me up.

I’d hit the gym every day for a week.

Then life would get in the way.

A busy day at work, or I’d get sick, and suddenly I’d miss a day or two.

That would turn into a missed week or two.

And then I’d fall right back into old habits and lose all the progress.

Here’s the 8 step system that enabled me to stay consistent with my workouts for more than 1.5 yrs:

1. Schedule workouts on your calendar for when you won’t have any distractions. I have a recurring calendar block to workout first thing in the morning every day.

2. Set reminders on your phone.

3. Find a workout buddy to keep you accountable. To kick start, I got a personal trainer for 3 months.

4. Have a morning ritual. Mine is - wake up to Eye of the Tiger (from Rocky), give thanks for something good that happened the previous day, bang out max number of pushups.

5. Leave your gym bag by your bed or somewhere visible.

6. Track your progress. Use an app like MyFitnessPal or pick one that you like. Every inch you drop, every ounce of muscle you add is a huge driver in staying consistent.

7. Journal in public. Another way to stay accountable. Plus the feedback and support from the community can be incredibly helpful in staying consistent as the encouragement and advice pours in.

8. Celebrate little wins. Smile every time you receive a compliment and tell them what you’ve been doing. At the very least, you might inspire someone else to do the same. And that creates a cycle of positive emotions helping you build momentum and achieve greater success in the long run.

Then you don’t have to rely on motivation alone - you have a routine that you're committed to, making it much easier to stick with it.

Last year upon leaving Boast, the company I co-founded, I was lost and clueless as I believed that the company was my identity.

After driving my closest relationships up the wall with my constant rants, I decided to make family and fitness my new focus.

Years of startup grind had taken a toll on my health plus COVID hospitalization nearly destroyed me in early 2021.

If I wanted to be able to run around with my kids, I would first need to get in shape.

Here are 11 things I did to drop to under 12% body fat from 22% & get into the best shape of my life:

1. Cut out negative energy

Avoid negative people, don’t watch the news, disengage from negative conversations and content

Kickstarted by killing social media for 5 mo

This had a profound impact on my mindset

2. Cut out dopamine

No alcohol, no caffeinated energy drinks, no vaping, no sugar (still the hardest)

3. Clean my diet

Eat protein rich whole foods, remove all processed items

4. Intermittent fast

I mostly fast 18-20 hours per day and eat within a 4-6 hour window

I tried a bunch of diets including 5-6 meals per day but always fell off the wagon

Fasting is not only sustainable for me but also keeps my energy levels high and my brains sharp

5. Walk 10-20,000 steps per day

Walking is the most underrated yet sustainable exercises you can do

I break this into 2-3 sessions

Listening to audio books, long walks with my wife, or walking meditation make walking something I look forward to every day

6. Lift weights

I keep it simple with just bench press, squat, shoulder press, deadlift, and row… Full body workouts 4-5x per week

Progressive overload aka lift a little heavier than the last time, do more reps than the last time, less rest b/w sets, drop set etc.

Progressive overload also gives you the high of unlocking new achievements

7. Have a challenge day every week

Max # of pushups, improve my 100 meter run timing, do a difficult workout like hand stand pushups etc

8. Sleep 7-8 hours… no two ways about this

9. Drink 2-3 litres of water every day

10. Have a morning ritual

Upon waking up, the first thing I do is give thanks for something good that happened in my life the previous day. Perhaps an event, an experience, a thing, or a person.

Example: last night I was thankful for an impromptu glorious South Indian meal followed by disco chai with my closest peeps - Vivi Lobo, Sachin Mendonca, Kenneth Daniel Monis, Alex Popa and Emmanuelle Popa.

11. Consistency

Here are 11 lessons I learned from Sridhar Vembu, Founder & CEO of Zoho, that will change the trajectory of your business:

Purpose - why do we exist?
Vision - what will the world look like because of us?
Mission - how do we do it?
Values - how do we behave?

2. Anything worth doing in life takes patience and perseverance.

3. In the early days stay laser focused on a small niche and only add new products, new markets, and new distribution channels when successful in that niche. 

4. Build strong customer relationships and invest in customer success to gain valuable feedback and improve products.

5. Focus on revenue-generating products and keep costs low to maintain profitability.

6. If you built a profitable company, you can be your own VC and invest in your own growth. Today Zoho is flush with just as much cash as public companies in their category.

7. Hire passionate and talented employees who share the company's values and foster a strong company culture that encourages innovation, collaboration, and creativity.

10. If you focus on POWER, you destroy relationships. If you focus on MONEY, you make short term decisions. The only way to build something lasting is to focus on IMPACT.

11. Giving your time and energy to help others without expecting anything in return will bring you great abundance.

Patrick Phat Nguyen posted: Làm Product, viết spec sao cho đủ technical? Dạo gần đây, mình nhận được khá nhiều câu hỏi: “Không technical thì viết spec như thế nào để Dev hiểu?”, hoặc “Spec đã viết kỹ lắm rồi mà Design vẫn thiếu screen, QA vẫn thiếu case, còn Tech thì bảo chưa đủ technical?” Theo mình, vấn đề không hẳn nằm ở việc PM không biết code. Phần lớn vấn đề do PM chỉ mới mô tả ở tầng UI (màn hình có gì, user bấm vào đâu) nhưng chưa mô tả được hệ thống ở tầng Concept (thực thể gì, thuộc tính nào, và vận hành ra sao) Vấn đề này có 3 triệu chứng sau: (1) Bắt đầu bằng màn hình thay vì đối tượng nghiệp vụ: có flow và UI, nhưng chưa rõ dữ liệu, trạng thái và logic phía sau. (2) Mô tả ở tầng trừu tượng quá thấp (Quá technical) khiến team không hiểu feature sẽ generalize như thế nào, hoặc ở tầng trừu tượng quá cao (Quá high-level), không biết feature sẽ hành xử cụ thể ra sao. (3) Không thể mô tả và tư duy edge case của tính năng mà chỉ mô tả được happy case.
